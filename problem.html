The Problem: The Water Crisis

The Problem

Fresh water scarcity is one of the most pressing and concerning problems the world is facing. An estimated nine hundred million people lack access to fresh water. Most of the people suffering from the water shortage reside in developing countries in Africa, Asia and Latin America.

The Water Shortage

The water shortage has severe consequences including fatal health consequences, diminished opportunity for work and education, which continue the cyclical poverty and disenfranchisement. The growing competition to consume the limited availablity of fresh water will contribute to the unrest in these developing countries. Ultimately, this will become a global issue.
